Quick Response Builder

Conversation Checklist

  1. Listen and clarify the real objection

    Ask a clarifying question first so you answer the person’s actual concern, not a different one.

    Example: “Is your biggest concern evidence, hypocrisy, or suffering?”

  2. State the Christian claim in one sentence

    Lead with a concise thesis before giving supporting reasons.

    Example: “Christians believe Jesus truly rose from the dead in history.”

  3. Give one reason + one Scripture anchor

    Use one strong point at a time and attach one key passage rather than overwhelming with too much information.

    Example: “One key reason is the early eyewitness testimony in 1 Corinthians 15.”

  4. Invite dialogue and offer a trusted next step

    End with humility, ask a follow-up question, and share one reliable Protestant resource link.

    Example: “Would you be open to reading this short article together and discussing it?”
